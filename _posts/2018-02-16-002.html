Sanity

Writing is a record of my sane part. But it might not be able to maintain that part in my thought. I put letters side by side to reassure that I can still make sense of something. The hardest part, then, is not what to write but how to write. This may sound very similar to Ryman's questions about painting. The subject for writing is not a representation of the writer. But I still don't know if I truly believe my argument. I'm searching for my white paint, the subject that neutral enough to be used without much interference with the very act of writing. Some entities, e.g. every/some, thing/nothing, ever/never1, are interesting becuase of their ambiguity. They are comparable to the measurement of space. In cosmic scale, inner space is our world, and outer space is the infinite horizon outside. Ever and never also acts as an opposition between positive and negative space; something inside and something outside. However, something and everything related to each other in a different way. Even they are entities to indicate space, everything covers something inside it, while something and nothing occupied different space, though their overlapping border is problematic.
