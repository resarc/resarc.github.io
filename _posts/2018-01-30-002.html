In the Name of …

Main actor and actress has been stressed significanty. It is often asked "who play the main characters?" rather than "what is the story about?" But the problem is not in this questioning, it is in the reaction after acknowledged. And I've missed the good ones because I don't like some leading actors.

The separation lies in the way of naming. On the one hand, they are being named in English, this group has something in particular: their casts are comparatively younger. On the other, their names are in Thai which are rather hard to translate into English, so they are generally use transliteration. The most obvious reason is their interest in reaching more varied audiences.

Logos are often provided with English-named series. And they are being made in the same mindset of advertising. Costumes and props are well chosen. Even their stories can be surreal, it has to be grounded on a real reality.

Writing is deleting. And sometimes it take more deletings to make a writing. Similar to Fred Sandback, planes are made out of lines rather than a surface. His 1999 statement1 brings a stoic voice to his work, while his statement in 19751 gives more precise explanation about spatial and temporal aspect in his sculptures (assuming from his own words that he'd like to be called "sculptor").

"I can’t isolate the pieces by letting them have any sort of modulation of time. Space can be modulated extensively in a way that does not imply temporal modulations. The way you arrive at a notion of the way anything is, as opposed to how it appears, is by constructing that notion in time—putting some things together at the expense of not seeing some other things."

And, of course, writing and deleting is not the opposition.
